Terms in Narsese, a formal language

Package Specification

NARS 4.3 implements the following formal language, Narsese. 
           <sentence> ::= <judgment>
                        | <question>
                        | <goal>
           <judgment> ::= <statement> <truth-value>
           <question> ::= <statement>
               <goal> ::= <statement> <desire-value>
          <statement> ::= <<term> <relation> <term>>
                        | <compound-statement>
                        | <term>
               <term> ::= <word>
                        | <variable>
                        | <compound-term>
                        | <statement>
           <relation> ::= -->
                        | <->
                        | {--
                        | --]
                        | {-]
                        | ==>
                        | <=>
                        | =/>
                        | =\>
                        | </>
 <compound-statement> ::= (-- <statement>)
                        | (|| <statement> <statement>+)
                        | (&& <statement> <statement>+)
                        | (&/ <statement> <statement>+)
                        | (/> <statement>)
                        | (\> <statement>)
      <compound-term> ::= {<term>+}
                        | [<term>+]
                        | (& <term> <term>+)
                        | (| <term> <term>+)
                        | (- <term> <term>)
                        | (~ <term> <term>)
                        | (* <term> <term>+)
                        | (/ <term>+ _ <term>*)
                        | (\ <term>+ _ <term>*)
           <variable> ::= <dependent-var>
                        | <independent-var>
                        | <query-var>
      <dependent-var> ::= #<word>(<independent-var>*)
    <independent-var> ::= #<word>
          <query-var> ::= ?<word>
               <word> : string in an alphabet
        <truth-value> : a pair of real numbers in [0, 1] x (0, 1)
       <desire-value> : a pair of real numbers in [0, 1] x (0, 1)
